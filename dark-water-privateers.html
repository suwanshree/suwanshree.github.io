Dark Water Privateers

Dark Water Privateers is a community site for a gaming organization, built to support recruitment, shared identity, and a future in-game asset tracker. The current build is a polished static foundation hosted on Vercel, with room to grow into a more capable full-stack member tool. Preview it at dwpllc.vercel.app.

Designing for the Present and the Roadmap

This project balances two needs at once: it has to function today as a strong public-facing site, and it also has to suggest where the product is headed. That meant creating a front end with enough clarity and modularity to support later expansion into authenticated tools and shared asset management without rebuilding the entire experience from scratch.

Focus: Community storytelling, scalable front-end structure, and product roadmap thinking.
Stack: Next.js, Vercel, and a static-first architecture designed for future feature growth.
What it shows: An ability to shape a credible first release while planning ahead for a more ambitious platform.

Why It Matters

Dark Water Privateers is a good example of pragmatic product thinking. Not every project needs a database on day one, but strong information architecture, brand tone, and extensible front-end decisions still matter. This work reflects how I approach MVPs with both discipline and imagination.
